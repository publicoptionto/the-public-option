I’m writing to encourage you to support Councillor Anthony Perruzza’s proposal to pilot city-run grocery stores in Toronto.

For many residents, the cost of groceries has become a growing source of stress. Prices for basic food items continue to climb, and too many people are finding it harder to keep up. While there isn’t a single solution to food affordability, Perruzza’s pilot offers a constructive way for the city to explore whether a municipally run option could help improve access to affordable groceries.

A pilot program would allow Toronto to test this idea in a practical way, learn from the results, and evaluate whether it could help address gaps in the current food retail system. Even on a limited scale, the program could provide valuable insight into how cities might play a role in supporting food security and price stability for residents.

At a time when affordability is a major concern for many households, it’s important that the city is willing to examine new approaches. I hope you will support giving this pilot the opportunity to move forward.

Thank you for your time, and I look forward to hearing your thoughts.
